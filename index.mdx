Sentinel is the platform for putting robots to work.

You bring a robot. Sentinel gives you everything around it: a way to drive it, the people who operate it, the data it produces, and the tools to run a growing fleet.

## Start with teleoperation

Everything begins with a person driving a robot.

Sentinel runs on the computer next to your robot. One file tells it what your robot can do. From there, an operator puts on a headset and drives — in the same room, or on the other side of the world. What they see is live. Not video-call live, actually live.

## Grow into an operation

Deploying a robot is a journey, and most teams walk the same path.

1. Teleoperate. Drive the robot in person. Try tasks. Learn what is actually doable.
2. Collect. Operators run tasks remotely. Every session they run is already a dataset, labeled and ready for training.
3. Automate. Models take over the routine work. People watch, step in when something needs a hand, and every intervention makes the next model better.
4. Scale. More robots, more operators, one place to see all of it.

Sentinel is built so that moving to the next stage never means switching platforms. The robot you teleoperate on day one is the robot your models run on later.

## Three ways to control a robot

Every robot on Sentinel can be controlled from three directions.

- From your code. Your robot speaks ROS 2, and Sentinel maps onto the topics you already publish.
- By a person. Operators connect from a headset and drive with their hands.
- Over the network. An HTTPS call can arm a robot, start a session, or send it home.

Whichever surface you use, it is the same robot, the same state machine, and the same safety rules underneath.

## What runs where

Sentinel is three pieces.

The runtime runs on the computer next to your robot. It talks to your hardware, streams video, records data, and enforces safety. It is one container and one config file.

The cloud connects everyone. It knows your robots, your operators, and your sessions, and it carries the connection between them.

The operator app runs on a headset. Today that means a Meta Quest 3. An operator signs in, picks a robot, and drives.
